NEUROLOGIC MUSIC THERAPY

What is neurologic music therapy
• Neuroscience approach to Music Therapy
• Based on scientific knowledge in music perception, music production and the effects of nonmusical brain and behaviour functions
• Research - based system of 20 standardized clinical techniques for:
	• Sensorimotor training
	• Speech and language training
	• Cognitive training


NMT Training
• Neurologic Music Therapy training is issued by the Robert F. Unkefer Academy for Neurologic Music Therapy.
• NMT training allows the board-certified music therapist to practice and use the designation of Neurologic Music Therapist (NMT) for three years.
• The NMT Academy training is endorsed by the World Federation of Neurorehabilitation (WFNR), the European Federation of Neurorehabilitation Societies (EFNS), and by the International Society for Clinical Neuromusicology (CNM). NMT Academy.


Background
• Formally established in 1999/2000.
• Researchers and clinicians in music therapy, neurology, rehabilitation and brain sciences classified evidence into a system of standardized therapeutic techniques for sensorimotor, speech/language and cognitive rehabilitation.
• Dr. Michael Thaut (Founder) currently at U of T

Sensorimotor (i.e., TIMP)
Therapeutic Instrumental Music Performance
Uses musical instruments to facilitate functional movement patterns
Exercises targeted to improve range of motion, endurance, and fine motor control

Sensorimotor (i.e., RAS)

Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation
Uses rhythmic cues to facilitate gait training and motor recovery
Particularly effective for patients with Parkinson's Disease and stroke survivors

Cognition (i.e., Musical Neglect Training)

Visual Spatial Skills
Executive Functioning Skills
Uses musical exercises to address unilateral neglect
Engages attention and spatial awareness through active music making

Speech & Language (e.g., Melodic Intonation Therapy (MIT) & Therapeutic singing)
Primary tools: Rythm, pitch, melody
MIT uses melodic patterns to facilitate verbal expression in non-fluent aphasia patients
Therapeutic singing supports breath control, articulation, and vocal projection
Helps re-engage neural pathways for speech production

In Summary

• Neurologic Music Therapy is an evidence-based practice rooted in neuroscience

• Standardized techniques address sensorimotor, cognitive, and speech/language goals

• NMT training equips music therapists to work effectively with neurologic populations

• The field continues to grow with ongoing research and clinical application
