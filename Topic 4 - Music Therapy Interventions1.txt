Introduction

Each music therapy session is unique to the client.

Music therapists draw upon a collection of music therapy interventions
based on their own training, skill-set, and the needs of the client.
Overview
• Facilitated Music Listening
• Singing
• Lyric Analysis

• Song writing & Improvising
  (separate webinars)
Interventions: Diagnosis

• Most interventions can be implemented regardless of age or diagnosis,
  but some interventions lend themselves more toward certain goals than
  others.
                Music therapy interventions are different from
Interventions   music therapy approaches.
vs. Approach
                A music therapy approach is a method of
                working that adheres to specific concepts and
                tools.


                A music therapy intervention is the specific act
                of music making (ie., improvising) that is
                implemented to work toward a specific health
                care goal.
Group or Individual?

• Any of these interventions can be facilitated within a group or individual
  music therapy session.

Summary
• Music Therapy Interventions are
  implemented to reach specific health
  care goals

• No intervention is better than another

• Interventions are applicable to all
  ages and diagnoses, but not all health
  care goals.

• Most music therapy sessions use
  several interventions throughout a
  session.
