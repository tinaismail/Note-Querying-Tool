HISTORY OF MUSIC THERAPY IN CANADA
Mid-1950s:
Music therapists were working independently in Canada.
Examples include Fran Herman in Toronto, Norma Sharpe in St. Thomas, and Thérèse Pageau in Montreal.

In 1960:
Norma Sharpe conducted a survey of music in hospitals across Canada.
She discovered that many hospitals used music and music activities and that many staff had musical training or backgrounds.
The survey provided a baseline measure for music therapists.
The survey increased awareness of music therapy in hospitals and institutions.

HISTORY OF MUSIC THERAPY IN CANADA (CONT’D)
Early 1970s: Norma Sharpe had regular contact with individuals and organizations across Canada who shared an interest in music therapy.
Sharpe presided over the first music therapy conference at St. Thomas Psychiatric Hospital in St. Thomas, Ontario.

Mid-1970s:
Conferences were held to unify those working in the field of music therapy throughout Canada.
Conferences were used to share techniques, programs, and assessments of rehabilitative effectiveness.
These efforts led to the formation of a national association, the Canadian Music Therapy Association (1974).

MUSIC THERAPY IN EUROPE
Although musicians engaged with soldiers in hospitals during World War II, this was not seen as the founding motivation for music therapy in Europe.
Juliette Alvin, a cellist, worked with children with autism and individuals with developmental differences.
She founded the Society for Music Therapy and Remedial Therapy in 1958, later renamed the British Society for Music Therapy (BSMT).
The first training program was established at Guildhall in 1968.
The spread to other countries resulted in the European Association in 1989.
Music therapy was recognized by the British government in 1976 through the Association of Professional Music Therapy, now known as BAMT.

By the 1970s, music therapy was established in the Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Germany, Austria, France, Switzerland, Yugoslavia, and Belgium.

THE EUROPEAN ASSOCIATION OF MUSIC THERAPY (1989)
By 2005, the association also included Italy, Spain, Cyprus, Estonia, Finland, Greece, Hungary, Israel, Lithuania, Malta, Poland, Portugal, Switzerland, and the Republic of San Marino.

MUSIC THERAPY WORLDWIDE
Music therapy began in the United States in 1950 and soon spread across the globe.
Music therapy is practiced in the Americas, Europe, Australia, New Zealand, India, China, and Japan.
Historically, music therapists trained in either England or the USA and returned to their home countries to practice and establish training programs.
The profession has been heavily influenced by Western music and culture.
In some countries, leaders in music therapy visited and supported the development of the profession.
