**Summary of Key Points:**
- The World Health Organization defines health holistically, including mental and social well-being, not just the absence of illness.
- Mental health involves realizing one's abilities, coping with stress, working productively, and contributing to community.
- There is a distinction between mental health (a state of well-being) and mental illness (specific diagnosable conditions).
- Music is a normalized part of daily life, which may support its therapeutic application.
- The choice of music therapy approach depends on the client, reason for referral, and specific goals (group vs. individual).
- The references provided include studies on the economic burden of mental illness, the neurochemistry and brain activity related to music, and physiological effects of music-making (e.g., on immune markers and stress hormones).

What is Mental Health?
There is no health without mental health

Definition of health by the World Health Organization: "Health is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity."

Mental health is an integral part of this definition.

---

Mental health vs mental illness

---

Mental Health
Mental health can be conceptualized as a state of well-being in which the individual
- Realizes their own abilities
- Can cope with the normal stresses of life
- Can work productively and fruitfully
- Is able to make a contribution to their community
